This document provides exact search queries for finding papers on Pareto optimization, visualization, carbon footprint, and design automation across Google Scholar, IEEE Xplore, and ScienceDirect.

## Overview

**Total papers:** 200
**Duration:** 3 weeks
**Search phases:** 15 (5 per week × 3 weeks)
**Databases:** Google Scholar (primary), IEEE Xplore (secondary), ScienceDirect (tertiary)
**Reading pace:** 5-8 papers/day

---

## Search Databases & Tips

### Google Scholar
- **URL:** https://scholar.google.com
- **Best for:** General discovery, citation counts, recent papers
- **Pro tip:** Sort by "recent first" for latest research, "by citation count" for seminal papers
- **Search operators:** Use quotes for exact phrases: `"pareto frontier"`

### IEEE Xplore
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org
- **Best for:** Engineering-specific papers, conferences, standards
- **Pro tip:** Filter by "Content Type: Conferences" or "Journals"
- **Search operators:** AND, OR, NOT for Boolean searches

### ScienceDirect
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com
- **Best for:** Elsevier journals, broad interdisciplinary coverage
- **Pro tip:** Filter by publication date (last 10 years)
- **Access:** Check if your institution has access

## Search Tips & Tricks

### Google Scholar Tips
- **Free access:** Use university/library proxy or ResearchGate to find PDFs
- **Citation tracking:** Click "Cited by X" to find newer papers on same topic
- **Related articles:** Click "Related articles" to find similar papers
- **Alerts:** Set up email alerts for new papers in specific areas
- **Sorting:** Sort by "recent first" or "citation count" depending on need

### IEEE Xplore Tips
- **Filter wisely:** Use Content Type filters to focus on Conferences or Journals
- **Boolean search:** Use AND, OR, NOT for precise queries
- **Publication date:** Filter to last 10 years for recent research
- **Open access:** Filter "Open Access" to get free PDFs

### General Tips
- **PDF repositories:** Check ResearchGate, Academia.edu for author PDFs
- **Preprints:** Check arXiv.org for computer science / optimization papers
- **Grey literature:** Check theses, technical reports, conference proceedings
- **Citation management:** Use Zotero, Mendeley, or Notion to organize papers
- **Quality check:** Prefer peer-reviewed journals over blog posts or grey literature
